Our Founder

The visionary behind our nation's independence.

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

As the leader of the All India Muslim League and the driving force behind the creation of Pakistan, Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah is revered as the Father of the Nation. His determination, leadership, and unwavering commitment to the cause of independence were instrumental in establishing Pakistan as a sovereign state in 1947.

Gallery

Memorable moments and historic events.

Quaid-e-Azam

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founding father of Pakistan, was a visionary leader who championed the cause of Muslim rights in the subcontinent. His steadfast leadership and determination were instrumental in the creation of Pakistan. Jinnah's principles of unity, faith, and discipline continue to inspire the nation. He remains a symbol of resilience and dedication to this day.

Abdul Sattar Edhi

Abdul Sattar Edhi was a revered humanitarian known for his unmatched dedication to helping the needy. As the founder of the Edhi Foundation, he established the largest and most respected social welfare organization in Pakistan. Edhi's efforts provided shelter, healthcare, and support to millions. His life is a testament to selflessness and compassion.

Allama Iqbal

Allama Muhammad Iqbal, a poet, philosopher, and visionary, was the spiritual father of Pakistan. His poetry and ideas played a crucial role in inspiring the movement for an independent Muslim state. Iqbal's vision of self-awareness and empowerment continues to resonate with people. His works are a source of inspiration and a beacon of hope.

Fatima Jinnah

Fatima Jinnah, affectionately known as Madar-e-Millat (Mother of the Nation), was a key figure in the Pakistan Movement. A close advisor to her brother, Quaid-e-Azam, she played a significant role in the struggle for independence. Fatima Jinnah also advocated for women's rights in the newly formed nation. Her legacy as a leader and a patriot remains strong.

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan, widely regarded as the father of Pakistan's nuclear program, played a pivotal role in making Pakistan a nuclear power. His work ensured the country's security and positioned it as a formidable force in the global arena. Khan's contributions to science and national defense have left an indelible mark on Pakistan's history. He is celebrated for his dedication to the nation.

Sir Syed Ahmad Khan

Sir Syed Ahmad Khan was a visionary leader who emphasized the importance of education for Muslims in British India. He founded the Aligarh Muslim University, a pivotal institution that produced many leaders of the Pakistan Movement. Sir Syed's efforts in modernizing education and promoting social reforms laid the groundwork for Muslim empowerment. His legacy is enduring in the educational landscape of Pakistan.

Khawaja Nazimuddin

Khawaja Nazimuddin was the second Prime Minister of Pakistan, taking office after the assassination of Liaquat Ali Khan. He played a critical role in stabilizing the country during its early years. Nazimuddin's commitment to democracy and his efforts to uphold the rule of law were pivotal in shaping Pakistan's parliamentary system. His leadership during a challenging time is remembered with respect.

Liaquat Ali Khan

Liaquat Ali Khan was Pakistan's first Prime Minister and a key figure in the Pakistan Movement. He was a close confidant of Quaid-e-Azam and played an essential role in the formation of Pakistan. Liaquat's leadership during the formative years of the country was crucial in setting the direction for the new nation. His tragic assassination left a void in Pakistan's political landscape.

General Tikka Khan

General Tikka Khan, known as the "Tiger of Punjab," served as Pakistan's Chief of Army Staff. He played a significant role in the 1965 and 1971 wars, demonstrating strategic military leadership. Tikka Khan's contributions to Pakistan's defense have earned him a prominent place in the country's history. His legacy is remembered for his dedication to national security.

Chaudhry Rehmat Ali

Chaudhry Rehmat Ali was the visionary who coined the name "Pakistan" for the proposed Muslim state. His pamphlet, "Now or Never," articulated the demand for a separate homeland for Muslims in India. Rehmat Ali's ideas laid the ideological foundation for the creation of Pakistan. His contribution to the nation's identity is immortalized in history.
